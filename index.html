About

I am a PhD candidate at the University of Kentucky working at the intersection of artificial intelligence, biomedical data science, medical imaging, and bioinformatics.

My work centers on developing interpretable and clinically relevant deep learning methods for complex biomedical problems, including multimodal imaging analysis, gene expression modeling, omics-based disease detection, and computational biology.

I am especially interested in methods that go beyond raw predictive performance and offer clarity, scientific insight, and practical value in real biomedical settings.
